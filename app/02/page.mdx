# Growth, Atlassian Intelligence
<span className="date">Jan, 2024</span>

Following the launch of Atlassian Intelligence, the focus shifted from shipping AI capabilities to driving meaningful adoption and long-term retention.

I led the design direction for the next phase of the Editor AI experience, redefining AI from a reactive command system into a proactive writing assistant embedded directly into user workflows.

---

## My role

As Lead Product Designer, I defined the UX vision for the next generation of AI experiences in the Editor. I partnered with product, engineering, and research to align strategy with execution, and drove the interaction patterns for proactive AI assistance.

## Problem definition

The team aligned around a focused opportunity: knowledge workers struggle with written communication, reducing their effectiveness and productivity. This reframed AI as a writing assistant rather than a collection of disconnected features.

## Design principles

Three principles shaped every decision. AI should be **proactive** — knowing when it can help without being asked. It should be **contextual** — keeping users in their existing workflow rather than pulling them out of it. And suggestions should always be **optional and transparent**, keeping the user in control.

## Design direction

I introduced the concept of unbundling the AI experience. Individual prompts became purpose-built product features, AI capabilities were embedded directly into existing editor foundations, and interactions shifted from reactive commands to contextual suggestions.

In practice this meant writing improvement integrated into editing workflows, suggested titles generated automatically from document changes, tone management elevated to a document-level goal, and grammar assistance surfaced through toolbar controls and settings.

---

## Outcome

Contextual prompts for page titles and writing improvement doubled usage. Narrowing the focus to writing assistance reduced confusion and improved qualitative feedback, while the proactive interaction model we established influenced AI product strategy well beyond the Editor.

> "Love it that I do not need to click a summarize button anymore."
